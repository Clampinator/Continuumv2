# Initial Concept

A TTRPG tool within a FoundryVTT system for tracking the Eras, Experiences and Events within the life of a time-traveling character.

# Continuum: Roleplaying in the Yet (Foundry VTT System)

## Product Vision
A specialized Foundry VTT system designed to automate the complex "Chronarchy" (time-travel record-keeping) of the Continuum RPG. This system empowers players and Game Masters to manage non-linear character progression with precision, clarity, and historical depth.

## Target Audience
- **Continuum Players & GMs**: The core user base requiring deep, lore-accurate mechanics for time travel.
- **New Time-Travelers**: Players who find the manual overhead of time-travel record-keeping a barrier to entry.
- **Foundry VTT Community**: Developers and power users interested in sophisticated, non-linear timeline systems.

## Core Goals
1. **Automate Chronarchy**: Eliminate the manual overhead of tracking a character's history, ensuring data consistency and preventing timeline paradoxes ("The Yet").
2. **Visual Spanning Tools**: Provide intuitive visual representations of a character's journey through time, clearly showing how "Span" (time-travel capability) is utilized.
3. **Immersive Historical Context**: Seamlessly bridge a character's events with spatial tracking across different eras.

## Key Features
- **Lifeline Spreadsheet**: A powerful, data-rich interface for managing character "Eras," "Experiences," and "Events," featuring "compensators" to maintain historical integrity.
- **Visual Span Graph**: A dynamic graphical interface that renders a character's lifespan and time-jumps as a visual "journey."
- **SpaceTime Map Bridge**: Integration with Foundry VTT's map engine to link events to specific locations and eras, providing a spatial dimension to chronological data.

## Aesthetic & Vibe
- **Historical-Tech Blend**: A unique UI aesthetic that combines the feel of high-tech chronometric instrumentation with the gravitas of a historical archive. It should feel both futuristic and deeply rooted in the past.
