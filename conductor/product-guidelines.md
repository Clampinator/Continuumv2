# Continuum VTT System Guidelines

## Documentation & Prose Style
- **Simple & Clear**: All documentation, tooltips, and in-game text must be easy to understand and follow. Avoid overly dense or complex language, especially when explaining non-linear concepts.
- **Goal-Oriented**: Focus on how the user can achieve their objectives (e.g., adding an event, checking for 'Frag').

## User Experience (UX) Principles
- **Data-Rich & High-Density**: The system must prioritize information density, especially for the lifeline spreadsheet and span graph, ensuring all critical data is accessible at a glance.
- **Streamlined Interaction**: Minimize the number of clicks required for common actions like adding time-jumps or editing events. Use keyboard shortcuts where appropriate.
- **Visual Clarity & Navigation**: Ensure clear visual feedback for all user actions. The non-linear nature of the character's history must be easy to navigate and visualize.

## UI Component Style
- **Modular & Functional**: Break down complex interfaces into clear, manageable sections. Components should be designed for reuse and consistency across different actor sheets (Character, Organization, Location).
- **Data-Driven Sections**: Organize data into logical groups that match the 'Chronarchy' workflow (e.g., Eras, Experiences, Events).

## Error & Feedback Messaging
- **Thematic & Lore-Aware**: Use game-specific terminology (e.g., 'Frag', 'The Yet', 'Span Pool') to provide contextually relevant feedback.
- **Informative Alerts**: When an action might lead to a paradox or invalid timeline, clearly explain the issue and provide actionable steps to resolve it.
